Design Process & Iterations

Our iterative engineering approach helped us refine sealing performance, water flow behavior, and modularity across multiple rounds of testing.

Round 1 – Concept & Ideation

Initial brainstorming focused on making hydroponics more natural, modular, and visually calming— using bamboo as both structural inspiration and aesthetic anchor.

Round 2 – Early Sketching

Mengyuan Xiong introduced magnet-based module connections and tension cables for secure alignment.

Round 3 – Water Storage Strategy

Josh Kirby proposed a basin-based reservoir to improve maintainability and support multiple linked modules.

Round 4 – End Cap Experiments

We tested sloped vs. non-sloped geometries, gasket fits, and bamboo-splitting behavior.

Round 5 – Nozzle Mechanism

Multiple nozzle iterations explored leak prevention, tube tightness, and spring-based actuation.

Round 6 – O-Ring Water Sealing

Integrating O-rings significantly improved watertight performance during pressure changes.

Round 7 – Toward Automation

Experiments began on nutrient balance, oxygenation, germination, and lighting behaviors.

Reflection

Through repeated testing, we learned how small geometric changes affect sealing reliability, manufacturability, and system usability.

Challenges & Innovation

Challenges

  • Balancing oxygen, water, nutrients & light across modules
  • Ensuring watertight sealing despite bamboo diameter variations
  • Preventing bamboo splitting at connection points
  • Refining valve tolerances for consistent actuation
  • Germinating seeds directly inside the module
  • Automating light positioning & nutrient dosing
  • Reducing cost for broader accessibility

Future Innovation

Weight sensors per module could unlock advanced automation:

  • Measure water & nutrient consumption
  • Track evaporation & plant transpiration patterns
  • Detect plant growth stages from mass changes

Long-term, BambooFlow could integrate into home plumbing systems or smart-home networks, providing nearly hands-free indoor food production.
