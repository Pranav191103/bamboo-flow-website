BambooFlow ¬∑ FP1.4

Turning bamboo into a modular hydroponic farm.

BambooFlow explores how a familiar, calm material‚Äîbamboo‚Äîcan host a highly engineered hydroponic system that quietly circulates water, nutrients and oxygen through a chain of wall-mounted modules.

Focus End-cap sealing ¬∑ Valve design ¬∑ Water flow
Status Working prototype & early growth tests

What problem are we solving?

Many apartment dwellers want to grow greens but don‚Äôt have sunlight, space, or time to maintain soil-based plants. BambooFlow turns vertical wall space into a quiet, low-maintenance hydroponic system.

The core challenge was designing a reliable end-cap + push-nozzle valve that could seal against real, imperfect bamboo while still being easy to assemble and service.

Final FP1.4 prototype: two modules connected in series with a submersible pump circulating nutrient solution from a beaker reservoir.

üìê Engineering Iterations
Sketches ¬∑ CAD ¬∑ Prototypes ¬∑ Testing

The assignment rubric required careful documentation of design iterations. The galleries below show how the BambooFlow end-cap and push-nozzle evolved across sketches, Fusion 360 models, 3D prints, and hardware tests.

1 ¬∑ Early Sketches & Dimensional Planning

Sketch ¬∑ Diameter ranges
Laying out minimum and maximum bamboo diameters, gasket thickness and O-ring clearances. These dimensions later became reference sketches inside Fusion 360.
Sketch ¬∑ Valve mechanisms
Side-view sketches comparing different spring placements and travel distances for the push nozzle. The goal: enough travel to feel responsive but not so much that the gasket breaks its seal.
Sketch ¬∑ Spring & pipe ideas
Rough ideas for how a bellows or accordion gasket could compress as the end cap is tightened around irregular bamboo surfaces.

2 ¬∑ Fusion 360 Exploration

Fusion ¬∑ End-cap layout
Base sketch defining concentric rings for the bamboo wall, gasket pocket, and outer rim of the end cap, plus bolt holes aligned to the bamboo centerline.
Fusion ¬∑ Longitudinal section
Longitudinal section showing how the gasket pocket, bamboo wall, and nozzle shaft stack up. This was critical to prevent unprintable overhangs and to make sure the gasket had room to flex.
Fusion ¬∑ Planes & construction
Construction planes marking the three O-ring positions along the nozzle shaft. These drove consistent spacing across multiple nozzle versions.
Fusion ¬∑ Valve profile
Final valve profile in Fusion: a rounded nose for smooth insertion into tubing, a shoulder for O-ring seating, and flat faces for the spring contact ‚Äúears‚Äù.
Fusion ¬∑ Inserted into end cap
Push-nozzle inserted through the end-cap body with cutaway. This view made it easy to measure clearances between the nozzle and the silicone channel.
Fusion ¬∑ Full assembly
Full assembly of a bamboo module with both end caps, push nozzle, and the top plant opening. This helped validate that the mechanism stayed compact and visually quiet.

3 ¬∑ Nozzle & O-Ring Iterations

Prototype ¬∑ V1‚ÄìV3 nozzles
Three generations of the push nozzle. Early versions had narrow sealing surfaces and were hard to clean; later versions added stronger shoulders and simplified the cable tie interface for the springs.
Prototype ¬∑ V2 nozzle
V2 nozzle geometry: three posts for springs and a central shaft for O-rings. This print helped tune tolerances before adding the actual elastomer components.
Prototype ¬∑ V2 with O-rings
V2 nozzle with O-rings installed at three heights. Testing this version surfaced issues with friction and insertion force that informed the final geometry.
Detail ¬∑ O-ring selection
O-ring sizing experiments. We compared cross-section, durometer, and diameter to find a combination that sealed reliably without making the push action too stiff.

4 ¬∑ End-Cap Shell & Gasket Iterations

Prototype ¬∑ End cap v1
First 3D-printed end-cap for test bamboo. This version validated basic fit but lacked a dedicated channel for silicone and had sharp edges that created stress risers.
Prototype ¬∑ End cap v2
V2 end cap increased the gasket pocket depth and refined bolt hole spacing so the cap could clamp evenly on tubes with slightly different diameters.
Prototype ¬∑ End cap v3
V3 introduced smoother fillets and a more generous rim to protect the silicone edge from chipping when the cap is handled or overtightened.
Prototype ¬∑ Pre-final
Pre-final build before painting and gasket casting. At this stage we were mainly tuning screw access and wall thickness for long-term durability.
Prototype ¬∑ Painted with gasket
Painted end cap with cast silicone donut gasket. The paint color helps visually blend the printed part with the bamboo while still keeping the mechanism legible.
Prototype ¬∑ Gasket filled
Close-up of the silicone donut fully filling the pocket. Multiple casting trials were required to remove bubbles and get a clean sealing surface.

5 ¬∑ Springs, Hardware & Water Testing

Prototype ¬∑ Springs
Cutting stock extension springs into shorter segments to tune the push force and travel. We tested multiple combinations before settling on the final feel.
Prototype ¬∑ End cap & nozzle
End cap with the nozzle installed and springs anchored. This assembly confirmed that the spring geometry cleared the bamboo inner wall.
Prototype ¬∑ Final end-cap face
Final end-cap face with the inner push nozzle installed. The design balances visual simplicity with the mechanical needs of the pump connection and valve.
Test ¬∑ Pump in sink
Early pump testing in the sink to validate flow rate, head height and bubble generation before connecting to the bamboo modules.
Component ¬∑ Motor
Compact DC submersible pump used in the final system. Its small footprint made it easy to hide in a reservoir without distracting from the bamboo aesthetic.
Electronics ¬∑ Control board
Variable-speed driver and wiring harness. This allowed us to slow the flow for single modules and increase it for multi-module chains.

6 ¬∑ System Integration & Final Prototype

Assembly ¬∑ Bamboo + caps
All major components laid out before final assembly: bamboo shell, silicone gasket, end caps and push nozzle. This made it easy to inspect tolerances at each step.
Assembly ¬∑ Valve press
Verifying the push feel and spring return after the gasket was fully cured and the cap was bolted to the bamboo.
System ¬∑ Dual-module test
Two-module integration test verifying that the seals hold, flow remains stable, and the pipe-cleaner ‚Äúplants‚Äù stay visually upright as water circulates.
