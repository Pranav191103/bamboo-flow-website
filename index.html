From Idea to Working Prototype

We focused on both functionality and user experience‚Äîtesting how water flows, how seals behave, how modules connect, and how plants respond. Below is a summary of each major iteration and what we learned.

Round 1 ‚Äì Concept & Ideation

Brainstorming how bamboo could serve as both structural inspiration and aesthetic identity.

Round 2 ‚Äì Early Sketching

Explored magnet-based connections and tension cables for secure modular assembly.

Round 3 ‚Äì Water Storage Exploration

Transitioned from in-module water storage to a basin reservoir for easier maintenance.

Round 4 ‚Äì End Cap Iterations

Foam mockups, sloped and non-sloped 3D printed caps were tested for sealing and bamboo stability.

Round 5 ‚Äì Nozzle & Valve Mechanism

Improved sealing, tightening mechanisms, and flow control through multiple nozzle prototypes.

Round 6 ‚Äì Spring Valve Refinement

O-rings added to improve watertight integrity and reduce micro-leaks.

Round 7 ‚Äì Early Plant Growth

Tested germination inside the system and evaluated water/oxygen/nutrient balance.

Reflection

Small geometry changes drastically affected performance. With more time, we would refine automation, basin plumbing, and lower-cost manufacturing.

‚öôÔ∏è Challenges & Innovation
What We Learned & What‚Äôs Next

Key Challenges

  • Maintaining watertight seals across varying bamboo diameters.
  • Testing germination and continuous growth inside the module.
  • Designing valves and seals that balance manufacturability with reliability.
  • Ensuring modularity without compromising water pressure or flow.
  • Finding low-cost alternatives for large scale adoption.

Future Innovation

Integrating weight sensors in each module would enable continuous measurement of:

  • Water consumption
  • Nutrient uptake
  • Evaporation and plant transpiration
  • Growth-phase detection

Long-term, BambooFlow could automatically adjust nutrients and water levels, and integrate with home plumbing for near-hands-free operation.
